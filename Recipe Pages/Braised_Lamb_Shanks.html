Braised Lamb Shanks

Ingredients
3 1lb/450g rear lamb shanks
2 tablespoon vegetable oil
1 garlic head
4 shallots
1 lemon, Zest only
1 medium large carrot
2 celery stalks
2 tablespoon tomato paste
1 teaspoon ground cumin
1 sprig rosemary
2 tbsp olive oil
2 bay leaves
1 bottle red wine
1 cup port
5 cups beef or chicken stock
2 tbsp butter

Slurry

3 tsp cornflour
2 tbsp water
Parsley (chopped)


Directions

Season the shanks with salt and pepper. Heat the oil in a Dutch oven or similar over medium-high heat until it starts to shimmer. Place the shanks carefully in the oil and sear until brown on all sides, especially the wide cut end. Remove and set aside. Add the diced shallots, carrots, and celery and cook, stirring from time to time, until golden brown. Add the tomato paste, cumin, rosemary and cook until the tomato paste turns a deeper colour, about one minute. Deglaze with wine and port, stirring to release any pan fond. Reduce the wine by half, 4 to 5 minutes. Pour in the stock, add bay leaves and lemon zest, and bring to a simmer. Return the shanks to the pan along with any juices they may have released. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. Cover the pot and transfer it to a 350°F oven for 90 minutes. Meanwhile, cut the head of garlic in half width wise. Drizzle olive oil and wrap in foil. Place the garlic head in oven for 30 minutes. After 90 minutes, remove the lid. Add roasted garlic and mix into the liquid. Return to oven uncovered and finish braising the lamb until fork-tender, for another 40-60 minutes at 320°F. Strain the sauce and place in small saucepan. Bring to a simmer and scoop out any fat and scum. Thicken with corn flour slurry if required. Whisk in soft butter away from heat. Pour sauce covering the whole shank, from the bone down. Garnish with chopped parsley and fresh rosemary leaves.
