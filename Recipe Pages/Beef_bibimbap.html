Beef Bibimbap

Ingredients

Marinade

1 tbsp sugar
1 zucchini
4 oz button mushrooms
2 scallions
6 oz carrots
1 nub ginger
Â¾ cup jasmine rice
5 tsp white vinegar
1 tbsp sesame oil
4 tbsp soy sauce
2 tsp sriracha
10 oz ground beef


Directions

Cut zucchini into half-moon slices. Thinly slice mushrooms and scallions, seperationg whites and greens. Shave carrots into ribbons, tossing the core. Mince peeled ginger and garlic.

In small pot, combine rice, 1 1/4 cups water, and a pinch of salt. Bring to boil, then cover and reduce heat to low. Cook until rice is tender, about 15-18 minutes. Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.

In a small bowl, combine scallion whites with vinegar and a pinch of salt. Set aside to quick-pickle. In a seperate small bowl, combine sesame oil, half the soy sauce, 1 tbsp sugar, and sriracha to taste.

Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add carrots, salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until just tender, 3-4 minutes. Transfer to a bowl. Add zucchini and another drizzle of oil to the pan. Cook, stirring, until tender, 4-5 minutes. Transfer to bowl with carrots. Add mushrooms and another drizzle of oil to the pan. Cook, stirring, until tender, 3-5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to bowl with other veggies.

Heat another drizzle of oil in the same pan over medium-heat. Add garlic and ginger. Cook, stirring, until fragrant, 20-30 seconds. Add beef and cook, breaking up meat into pieces, until lightly browned, 3-4 minutes. Drain any excess grease from the pan. Increase heat to high and cook until browned, crisp, and cooked through, 2-3 minutes. Stir in remaining soy sauce and cook until mostly evaporated, 1-2 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.

Fluff rice with a fork; portion into bowls. Arrange beef, zucchini, carrots, and mushrooms on top. Top with pickled scallion whites (draining first). Drizzle with sauce and any remaining sriracha to taste. Sprinkle with scallion greens and serve.
